The 5-Second Beauty Hacks Supermodels Swear By (But Never Share)

Backstage at Fashion Week, where models transform in minutes between shows, makeup artists have perfected the art of instant glamour. We went undercover with the pros who prep faces for Marc Jacobs, Victoria's Secret, and Chanel to steal their most guarded shortcuts—the ones they reserve for A-listers rushing between fittings.

1. The Under-Eye Wake-Up Trick

What they do: Dab a dot of peach-toned corrector (not concealer) at the inner corner, then pat outward with a ring finger.

Why it works: The warmth neutralizes blue tones better than layers of product. Patric Mathews, lead artist for Dior, admits: "We use this on models who partied pre-show."

2. Natural-Looking Overdrawn Lips

The secret: Trace just outside your Cupid’s bow with a finger—not a pencil—then blot.

Pro tip: Supermodels blot with a tissue they’ve licked (saliva sets pigment like nothing else).

3. Shaving Gel as Pore Eraser

The shocker: A pea-sized amount of unscented men’s shaving gel (like Gillette Pure) patted on clean skin fills pores better than primers.

Backstage proof: Used at 3 consecutive Prada shows to create that "filtered skin" effect.

4. Mascara That Won’t Budge

The trick: Coat lashes normally, then immediately press a tissue against them for 5 seconds.

Science says: This removes excess product that causes smudging while locking in the base layer.

5. The Celebrity Blush Shortcut

What you’re doing wrong: Applying to apples of cheeks.

What they do: Smile, then place blush only on the highest point of cheeks—it lifts the face instantly.

Why These Work When Your Routine Doesn’t

These hacks bypass unnecessary steps. As Pat McGrath’s team explained: "Luxury beauty isn’t about products—it’s about precision placement." Each targets one specific concern with surgical accuracy.
