These 5 'Investment Pieces' Are Actually Depreciating in Your Closet

We've all fallen for the myth of the "timeless" wardrobe staple—those classic pieces we're told will last forever. But resale market data reveals a harsh truth: many of these so-called investments are quietly losing value and relevance. Here's what needs to go, and what truly deserves your money instead.

GO BACK

1. The Classic Beige Trench Coat

Why It’s Out: The traditional double-breasted, knee-length trench has become the uniform of corporate middle managers. Resale sites report a 60% drop in demand for vintage Burberry trenches since 2018.

What to Buy Instead: A cropped, single-breasted leather trench (Bottega Veneta’s new version is already a resale star).

2. The Nude Patent Pump

Why It’s Out: The "professional basic" now reads as dated as a BlackBerry. TheRealReal reports patent pumps resell for just 30% of retail value.

What to Buy Instead: A square-toe slingback with a slight platform (Amina Muaddi’s styles hold 90% value).

3. The Structured Satchel

Why It’s Out: Millennial pink killed the work bag. Even Smythson’s iconic styles now linger on resale sites for months.

What to Buy Instead: A soft, oversized tote with slouchy structure (Khaite’s leather versions are spiking in resale value).

4. The Cashmere Twin Set

Why It’s Out: Fast fashion dupes destroyed the luxury appeal. Vintage Chanel cardigans now sell for less than Zara’s knockoffs.

What to Buy Instead: A cropped, chunky knit vest (old Celine styles are reselling above retail).

5. The Naked Dress

Why It’s Out: The 90s slip dress is officially overworked. Depop reports a 75% increase in listed-but-unsold versions.

What to Buy Instead: A bias-cut satin midi (new brand The Line by K is dominating resale).

The New Rules of Investment Dressing

If it’s been "timeless" for 30+ years, it’s probably stale

Seek pieces with subtle modern twists on classics

Check resale value before buying—not after
