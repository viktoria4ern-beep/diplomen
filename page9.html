The 5 Sunglasses That Never Go Out of Style (and 3 That Already Have)

In the ever-changing world of fashion, some accessories stand the test of time while others fade into obscurity. Sunglasses are no exception—what was once cutting-edge can quickly become dated, while truly iconic designs remain relevant decade after decade. We've analyzed street style archives, celebrity sightings, and designer collections to identify the five frames that will always look chic and the three trends best left in the past.

The Timeless Five

Cartier's Aviators

The gold standard of sunglasses, Cartier's aviators have graced the faces of style icons from Jackie Kennedy to modern celebrities. Their secret? Perfect proportions—neither too large nor too small—and just enough gold detailing to feel luxurious without being flashy. Unlike cheaper versions, Cartier's frames maintain their shape and luster for years.

Ray-Ban Wayfarers

A rebel's favorite since the 1950s, the Wayfarer's thick acetate frames and slight cat-eye angle work on nearly every face shape. They've been reinvented countless times but always return to their original form—proof that some designs are simply perfect as they are.

Persol 714

Steve McQueen's signature frames remain the ultimate in understated cool. The distinctive arrow hinge and gradient lenses elevate these beyond ordinary sunglasses, making them equally at home with a suit or weekend jeans.

Celine's Round Frames

A modern classic, Celine's round frames strike the ideal balance between vintage and contemporary. Slightly oversized with thin metal rims, they're the sunglasses equivalent of a perfectly tailored white shirt—always appropriate, never boring.

Oliver Peoples' Gregory Peck

Named after the legendary actor, these frames embody old Hollywood glamour. The subtle brow bar and lightweight construction make them ideal for those who prefer refinement over trendiness.

The Overrated Three

Dior's Oversized Mirrored Lenses

Once the ultimate status symbol, these now look like a relic from the 2010s influencer era. The extreme proportions and flashy mirrored coatings date instantly, and their impracticality (good luck seeing indoors) makes them more costume than accessory.

Chanel's Logo-Emblazoned Frames

Nothing screams "trying too hard" like sunglasses plastered with interlocking Cs. These peaked in the early 2000s and now read as gauche rather than glamorous. True luxury doesn't need to announce itself so loudly.

Vintage-Style Tiny Frames

While the tiny sunglasses trend had its moment, these impractical, face-squishing frames now look more like a fashion victim's mistake than a style statement. If you can't actually see through them, what's the point?

How to Spot a Future Classic

The best sunglasses share certain qualities:

Comfortable enough to wear all day
Flattering on multiple face shapes
Minimal visible branding
High-quality materials that age well

When considering new frames, ask yourself: Would this look out of place in a photo from 20 years ago? Will it still look good in 20 years? If the answer is no, you're better off investing in one of the timeless five instead.
